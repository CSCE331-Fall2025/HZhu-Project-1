Past Volunteering

Senior Homes

Mostly during high school and junior high, I went to different senior homes volunteering. This typically consisted of playing music to them as I played the flute all throughout middle school, junior high, and high school. In high school, me and some friends would start Music: The Cure, a volunteer group aimed at improving the quality of life and interacting with the senior residents in nursing homes through music, with a focus on those with memory-related illnesses. Musical activities included solo performances, organized group performances, and caroling. Outside of music, there were opportunities in collaboration with the nursing homes to do other activities like crafts or holding an Easter egg hunt. Nursing Home

TAMU Build

During my freshman year, I volunteered for Build through a cultural organization. Build was started after the bonfire incident in 1999 that would lead to the tragic loss of Aggies on a special day. In honor of that tragedy, the organization helps build medical clinics using shipping containers, providing access to clinics in healthcare deserts and underprivileged areas. The volunteering varies, but typically involves working with tools and teams to help construct the clinics. Though sometimes the labor may be tough, the cause and goals stem from a genuine desire to help those in need while remembering the lives lost in 1999. More information can be found at https://www.buildtamu.com.

Animal Shelters

I have always loved animals growing up and enjoyed interacting with them. If you were to ask me if I was a dog or cat person, I would immediately say I am a dog person, not to say that I do not like cats as I also adore them. Given the credentials of the vet school at Texas A&M, there are also plenty of organizations that work with animals.
